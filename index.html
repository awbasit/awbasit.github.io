Research Projects

Abstract

Ghana’s cocoa industry faces challenges due to manual record-keeping, fragmented data systems, and the exclusion of farmers from the supply chains. Despite being the world’s second-largest cocoa producer, many farmers struggle with poverty and marginalization due to limited access to critical information. The reliance on paper-based systems leads to inefficiencies, errors, and lack of transparency, complicating efforts to track ethical sourcing of cocoa products. This project proposed leveraging blockchain technology as a solution to address these challenges. Blockchain offers a decentralized digital ledger that ensures secure and transparent record-keeping, allowing all participants in the cocoa supply chain, including farmers, to access, update, and verify information in real-time. This system integrates data across the entire supply chain, facilitating accurate tracking of cocoa products from farm to market. The project report explores how blockchain can streamline record-keeping, unify fragmented data systems, and create an inclusive digital supply chain.

Abstract

The objective of this project is to investigate the application of blockchain technology to enhance sustainability and equity in small-scale mining (SSM) in Tarkwa, Ghana, addressing its social and environmental impacts. The research employed a mixed-methods approach, including a comprehensive literature review, stakeholder consultations, and the development of an Ethereum-based blockchain prototype. This framework aims to ensure fair benefits for miners, promote sustainable practices, and improve transparency in the mineral supply chain. Major results indicate that blockchain can significantly enhance accountability and traceability in SSM operations, although challenges such as poor infrastructure and low digital literacy remain. The study concludes that the successful implementation of blockchain technology, combined with community engagement and policy reforms, is essential for fostering sustainable and ethical mining practices, ultimately contributing to a more equitable future for the small-scale mining sector in Ghana.

Abstract

Improving the effectiveness of network intrusion detection is crucial in defending against increasingly sophisticated cyber assaults. The goal of this project is to improve the intrusion detection system’s accuracy and efficiency by utilizing deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs). This project’s objective is to develop a strong framework that can detect and mitigate various types of network intrusions by utilizing deep learning, especially CNNs. To teach CNN models to recognize complex patterns linked to cyber risks, the models are trained on large datasets of network traffic. The CNN-based intrusion detection system provides quick threat identification and response capabilities through real-time monitoring and analysis features. Anticipated outcomes include improved detection accuracy, reduced false positives, and greater scalability for handling high network traffic volumes.

Abstract

This project optimises machine learning algorithms to predict hypothyroid disease. Hypothyroid disease is one of the most common health disorders in the endocrine system and the health consequences of failing to diagnose and treat it in a timely manner can be severe. Conventional diagnostic techniques often present themselves as misses to very mild thyroid function delicacies and can thus delay diagnosis. This project applies machine learning to enhance prediction and diagnosis of hypothyroidism, in its forms. The study utilized data from 1,000 different people with different health attributes, The dataset included 376 positive cases and 624 negative cases of hypothyroidism. and the machine learning techniques that were tested included the Random Forest, Decision Tree, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Logistic Regression (LR), and Gradient Boosting algorithms. These algorithms were evaluated for their accuracy, precision, and recall in predicting hypothyroid conditions. The methodology included data collection, preprocessing, and normalization to ensure high quality inputs for the models. Results demonstrated that machine learning models could significantly enhance the prediction and early diagnosis of hypothyroid disease, offering a more comprehensive and personalized approach than the existing methods. In conclusion, by integrating these machine learning techniques into clinical practice, the project aims to ensure timely and accurate diagnosis of hypothyroid conditions, ultimately improving patient care.

Abstract

This project presents the development of a multimodal model for the detection of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), integrating Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The primary objective is to improve the accuracy and efficiency of ASD detection by combining two distinct dataset modalities of facial images and behavioral data. The model employs CNNs to extract features from facial images and LSTMs to analyze sequential behavioral data, thereby capturing complex patterns indicative of ASD across these modalities. Additionally, the project incorporates security measures by utilizing Homomorphic Encryption Scheme to ensure the protection of data during the prediction process. The proposed approach aims to enhance the diagnostic capabilities for ASD by leveraging the strengths of both CNNs and LSTMs while ensuring data security

Abstract

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) have increased precision and productivity in the current big data era. Machine learning has indicated its robustness in geosciences, particularly rock-type classification. The issue of lithological classification in the traditional way has raised critical concerns and the need to curb the limitations it breeds, such as time consumption and subjective results. The gold mineralisation occurrence is structurally controlled in the district. It exhibits complex patterns and relationships that may not be readily discernible through traditional methods, leading to geologists missing out on discovering new resources or potential exploration targets. Consequently, this research attempts to create a predictive model by exploring the best machine-learning algorithms to predict rock types in the Obuasi Gold District using X-ray fluorescence (XRF) geochemical data. Comparative predictive modelling was established using four supervised classification algorithms: Gradient Boosting (GBoost), Adaptive Boosting (AdaBoost), Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Random Forest (RF). The acquired XRF data was integrated with the model using the Orange Data Mining software. The performance evaluation of the models indicated SVM as the best algorithm for deployment with a Classification Accuracy (CA) of 0.902. Therefore, ML algorithms have been a great tool in rock-type classification, whereby SVM emerged the best in the case of the Obuasi Gold District. However, it is encouraged to understand the geology of a particular area before employing the tool. The datasets must be balanced to yield good results and avoid model overfitting.
